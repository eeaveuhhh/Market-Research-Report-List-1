<p><p>Antithrombotic drugs are medications used to prevent the formation of blood clots, also known as thrombosis. These drugs are primarily used to treat conditions such as deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, atrial fibrillation, and myocardial infarction. They work by inhibiting the formation of clotting factors or by interfering with the clotting process itself.</p><p>The market for antithrombotic drugs is experiencing significant growth due to factors such as the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, increasing geriatric population, and the growing demand for oral anticoagulant drugs. Additionally, advancements in drug delivery techniques and the introduction of novel antithrombotic drugs are driving market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing the emergence of direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs), which offer advantages over traditional anticoagulants such as warfarin, including a lower risk of bleeding and more convenient administration. DOACs have gained popularity due to their use in stroke prevention for patients with atrial fibrillation and the treatment of venous thromboembolism.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has had a positive impact on the antithrombotic drugs market. As COVID-19 is associated with a higher risk of thrombosis, there has been an increased demand for antithrombotic drugs for both prevention and treatment purposes.</p><p>According to the provided information, the antithrombotic drugs market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.7% during the forecast period.
