<p><p>The egg oil market is characterized by different extraction methods, including liquid-liquid extraction and supercritical fluid extraction. Liquid-liquid extraction involves the use of solvents to remove oil from egg yolks, while supercritical fluid extraction utilizes high-pressure carbon dioxide to extract oil from the eggs. Both methods have their own advantages and applications in the market, with liquid-liquid extraction being more cost-effective and widely used, while supercritical fluid extraction is known for producing high-quality oil with minimal solvent residue.</p></p>
