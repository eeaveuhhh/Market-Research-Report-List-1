<p><p>Pressure tanks for hydrogen storage are classified into four types based on their design and materials. Type I tanks are constructed from high-strength steel and have the highest weight but offer the lowest cost. Type II tanks feature a steel liner wrapped with a composite material which reduces weight but increases cost. Type III tanks have a fully composite structure, offering further weight reduction but at a higher cost. Type IV tanks employ a plastic liner with a carbon fiber overwrap, providing the lightest weight and highest cost among the four types.</p></p>
