<p><p>Killed-type poultry vaccines are designed to protect poultry from various diseases. They can be categorized into different types. NDV (Newcastle Disease Virus) vaccines protect chickens from Newcastle Disease, a contagious viral infection that affects the respiratory, nervous, and digestive systems. Marek's Disease vaccines target Marek's Disease Virus, which causes tumors and weakens the immune system in chickens. IBD (Infectious Bursal Disease) vaccines protect against a highly contagious viral infection that affects young chickens' immune systems. Infectious Bronchitis vaccines target a respiratory disease caused by the infectious bronchitis virus. Influenza vaccines protect poultry from influenza viruses. There are also other specific vaccines available to protect against other diseases that affect poultry.</p></p>
