<p><p>Food preservation additives are divided into two main types: natural additives and artificial additives. Natural additives are derived from natural sources such as plants, animals, or minerals and are considered safe for consumption. They include ingredients like salt, sugar, vinegar, and rosemary extract. On the other hand, artificial additives are chemically synthesized compounds that are added to food to extend its shelf life or enhance its flavor, color, or texture. Examples of artificial additives include preservatives, colorings, and flavor enhancers. Both types of additives play a crucial role in prolonging the shelf life of food products and ensuring food safety.</p></p>
