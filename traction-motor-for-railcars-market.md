<p><ul><li>Synchronous Motor</li><li>Asynchronous Motor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Traction motors for railcars can be categorized into two main types: synchronous motors and asynchronous motors. Synchronous motors have a fixed magnetic field that rotates at the same speed as the motor itself, providing efficient and precise control over speed and torque. Asynchronous motors, on the other hand, do not have a fixed magnetic field, allowing for more flexibility in speed control. Both types of motors are commonly used in railcar traction systems, with each offering unique advantages depending on the specific application requirements.</p></p>
