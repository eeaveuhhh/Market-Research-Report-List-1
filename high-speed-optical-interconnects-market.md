<p><p>High-speed optical interconnects are used for transmitting data over long distances at high speeds. The market for these interconnects can be divided into two types: single-mode and multi-mode. Single-mode interconnects use a single path for the transmission of light, enabling high speeds over longer distances. They are mainly used in long-haul telecommunications and data centers. Multi-mode interconnects use multiple paths for light transmission, allowing for shorter distances but higher bandwidth. They are commonly used in local area networks and shorter-range applications where high bandwidth is crucial.</p></p>
