<p><ul><li>Spunbonded</li><li>Staples</li><li>Meltblown</li><li>Composite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>PP nonwoven fabric market is segmented into spunbonded, staples, meltblown, and composite types. Spunbonded nonwovens are made by spinning and bonding fibers together, providing strength and durability. Staples involve fibers being mechanically entangled for a softer feel. Meltblown nonwovens are created by blowing molten polymer onto a conveyor, producing fine fibers for filtration applications. Composite nonwovens combine two or more types of nonwoven fabrics for enhanced properties. Each type caters to different market needs and applications in industries such as healthcare, automotive, and construction.</p></p>
