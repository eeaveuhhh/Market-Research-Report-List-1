<p><p>Cranial orthoses are divided into two main types: active helmets and passive helmets. Active helmets are equipped with sensors and technology that actively monitor and correct head positioning and movement. They are often used in rehabilitation settings to aid in the recovery of head injuries or neurological conditions. On the other hand, passive helmets provide support and protection without any technological components. They are typically used for preventive measures or for mild head injuries where simple support is sufficient.</p></p>
