<p><p>Ethylene Propylene Rubber (EPR) is a type of synthetic rubber that is created by polymerizing ethylene and propylene monomers. There are two main types of EPR in the market: Ethylene Propylene Monomer (EPM) and Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer (EPDM). EPM is known for its excellent resistance to heat, oxidation, and ozone, making it ideal for applications in automotive seals and gaskets. EPDM, on the other hand, is even more versatile with added resistance to weathering, UV exposure, and chemicals, making it suitable for a wider range of applications including roofing membranes and automotive hoses.</p></p>
