<p><p>White Light Interference Microscopes use white light as the light source for interference measurements. There are various types of white light interference microscopes available in the market, including Diffraction Grating Interferometers, Vertical Scanning or Coherence Probe Interferometers, and White Light Scatter-plate Interferometers. </p><p>Diffraction Grating Interferometers use diffraction gratings to split white light into different colors, while Vertical Scanning or Coherence Probe Interferometers utilize vertical scanning techniques to measure interference patterns. White Light Scatter-plate Interferometers use scatter plates to produce interference fringes.</p></p>
