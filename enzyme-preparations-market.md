<p><p>Enzyme preparations are classified into six main types based on their specific functions: Oxidoreductases, Transferases, Hydrolases, Isomerases, Lyases, and Ligases. Oxidoreductases catalyze oxidation-reduction reactions, Transferases facilitate the transfer of functional groups between molecules, Hydrolases assist in hydrolysis reactions, Isomerases catalyze isomerization reactions, Lyases facilitate the removal or addition of groups to double bonds, and Ligases catalyze the joining of two molecules. Each type of enzyme preparation plays a crucial role in various biochemical processes and industries.</p></p>
