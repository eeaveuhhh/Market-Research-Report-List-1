<p><p>Automotive brake friction products can be classified into three main types: brake pads, brake shoes, and brake lining. Brake pads are the most common type of friction product, made from materials like ceramic, organic, or metallic compounds. Brake shoes are used in drum brake systems and are typically made of the same materials as brake pads. Brake linings are often used in heavy-duty applications and are composed of high-friction materials like asbestos or semi-metallic compounds. These products play a crucial role in providing the necessary friction for braking in vehicles.</p></p>
