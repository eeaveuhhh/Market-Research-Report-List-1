<p><p>Aquatic animal vaccines can be classified into two main types: injectable vaccines and dip vaccines. Injectable vaccines are administered through injections, typically into the muscle of the animal. This method is commonly used for larger aquatic animals such as fish and shellfish. Dip vaccines, on the other hand, are administered by immersing the animals in a bath containing the vaccine. This method is often used for smaller aquatic animals or those that are more sensitive to handling. Both types of vaccines help protect aquatic animals from various diseases and health threats.</p></p>
